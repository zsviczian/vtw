A picture speaks a thousand words

According to some sources, the human brain processes images 60,000 times faster than text. Even if this estimate seems exaggerated, very few would debate that humans are visual creatures.

Visual tools for thinking tap into your brain's vast visual processing powerhouse boosting your creativity and accelerating learning and understanding.

The workshop

The best way to learn a new skill is by getting your hands dirty. In the workshop, we will learn and practice visual thinking by jointly working our way through an actual book that will be selected by the group. In the workshop I will work alongside you, creating my own summary of the book, and lead you through the step-by-step process of fully digesting the book. You will create literature notes,

author your own book on a page summary,


and add visual permanent notes to your personal knowledge management system connecting these notes to your book on a page and to other notes in your knowledge graph.

The visual summarization skills you'll learn and practice during the workshop will enable you to create visual summaries including strategic narratives, conference highlights, study notes for training courses, and more.

Is the Visual Thinking Workshop for me?

You can't draw??

Don't worry, I can't draw either. I have developed a complete arsenal of tools and workflows to help me overcome my lack of sketching skills. I will share these during the workshop.

Is your dream to be a visual storyteller?

Developing the skill to visualize important concepts and summarize books into visual overviews will go a long way in developing your skill to tell stories supported by simple visuals.

Are you reading books?

If you "just" read a book without putting in the effort of visually summarizing it, you're only getting a fraction of the value out of the book. I don't summarize every book, but the books I do, I internalize much stronger. The creative effort of coming up with visualizations, and organizing the content on the page so I can tell "my" story of the book deepens my understanding and recall of the book.

Are you looking for new ways to be creative?

The effort of finding good visual representations is an excellent excercise in Lateral Thinking (the term invented by Edward de Bono). If you are interested in lateral thinking I have many articles on the subject on my blog.

You don't have a stylus?

While sometimes I prefer drawing with my stylus, I do the majority of my sketching with a mouse. If you have a pen and you are good at quick sketches you'll be able to capitalize on that. If you don't have a stylus, or you are not handy with a pen, don't worry, a mouse is just as good an option.

Are you a business professional?

When I'm not playing with my hobbies (i.e. developing the Obsidian Excalidraw plugin or publishing content on YouTube) I'm a CIO in my "professional life". I use visual thinking tools and visual summaries every day. These skills are transferable and essential for business just as well as for pleasure.
